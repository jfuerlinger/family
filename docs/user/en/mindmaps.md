# Mind maps

**Mind maps** help your family make decisions together, calmly: Where to go on vacation? Which pet? New car or a cargo bike after all? Instead of losing arguments at the kitchen table, you collect ideas, arguments, and open questions visibly in one place.

## Creating a mind map

1. Open **Mind maps** in the menu.
2. Tap **Create**.
3. Enter a title, e.g. "Summer vacation 2026", optionally with a short description.
4. The new mind map opens in the editor — the central **Topic** node sits in the middle.

## Node types

Every node in a mind map has a type, recognizable by its color and shape:

| Type | What for? | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Topic** | The central question or main subject | "Summer vacation 2026" |
| **Idea** | A suggestion or option | "Camping in Croatia" |
| **Pro** | An argument in favor | "Cheap & by the beach" |
| **Con** | An argument against | "Long drive" |
| **Question** | Something that still needs answering | "Will the dog fit in the car?" |

A structure emerges naturally: topic → ideas → pros/cons/questions for each idea.

## Adding nodes

1. Tap an existing node.
2. Choose **Add** and pick the type you want (Idea, Pro, Con, or Question).
3. The new node appears connected to the selected one — tap it and write your text.

You can drag nodes around freely to keep the map tidy.

## Editing labels

Double-tap (or double-click) a node to edit its text. Press Enter or tap elsewhere to confirm.

## Deleting nodes

Select a node and tap **Delete**. Any child nodes will be left dangling — tidy them up while you're at it if needed.

## Auto-save

There's nothing to save manually: **FamilyHub saves your mind map automatically** while you work. Close the editor and open the map again later — everything's still there, for the whole family.

> **Tip:** Mind maps belong to the whole family. Let everyone add their own pros, cons, and questions — then everyone feels part of the decision in the end.

> **Note:** If two people edit the same mind map at the same time, the last save may overwrite the other person's changes. It's best to take turns — live collaboration isn't built in yet.
